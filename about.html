About Me

My Story

I graduated from Penn State with degree in Materials Science and Engineering. I spent seven years after graduating college working for an industrial gearbox manufacturer. Though I did perform my duties as a Metallurgist, I was also given the opportunity to delve deep into other roles. I was part of the quality team, running root cause analysis and corrective action investigations. I also spent the last two years of my tenure creating 3D models and drawings for parts and assemblies and learning the application analysis side of the business.

In 2019, I decided that I wanted to apply the problem solving, organization and critical thinking skills I cultivated in my previous job to other products. I wanted to be able to engineer products and processes so that the everyday user could have a better experiences every day.

In my mid twenties, before I decided to make a career change, I started taking an interest in human health and psychology. I started reading about the brain, about how and why we sleep and behave, about the influence of nutrition and environment on our wellbeing. Science is making so much progress and when put in context, the negative impact of technology on all of us is staggering.

I think technology is improving so many aspects of human lives. I also think design could dampen some of the damage technology is causing.

I’m interested in using UX design to implement multidisciplinary research to improve the way people interact with the world.

I don’t want to be pigeonholed into the health industry, I want to help users achieve their goals and create products that support their general wellbeing without asking them to make a conscious effort to be “healthy”.
